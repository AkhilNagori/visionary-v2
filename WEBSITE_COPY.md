## The developer section (Open Sauce crowd / GitHub README hero)

> **It runs Claude.**
> SSH into your glasses. Pipe what they see into Claude Code or Codex. Every "app" on this device is a prompt — fork one, write your own, ship it as a Prompt Pack others install by QR. Three-function SDK: `capture()`, `listen()`, `speak()`. The rest is your imagination.

Bullets:
- Full root access — it's your hardware, actually
- Agent-ready: hooks for Claude Code, Codex, and any API-speaking model
- Prompt Packs: community app store where apps are text files
- Swap the model, swap the voice, swap the frame — everything's a file

## Positioning guardrails (internal — don't publish)
1. Lead ads with the *stack replacement* story; keep the accessibility origin one scroll down — it's the soul of the brand, not the whole market. "Started as glasses that read for blind students. Turned out everyone wants a second brain."
2. Say "your notes app / your maps app" in written copy, not trademarked names, unless it's clearly comparative commentary.
3. Never claim: medical device, always-on assistant (it's press-to-capture), phone replacement (it's a phone *escape*), or offline parity for voice features.
4. The one-sentence verbal pitch at the booth:
   **"Open-source AI glasses — everything you pull your phone out for, hands-free, $99, and it runs Claude."**
