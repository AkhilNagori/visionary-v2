# Visionary — Booth Demo Script & Preorder Playbook

## The 60-second demo loop

1. **Hook (5s):** "Want to see $60 glasses read anything out loud? Put these on." Hand them the glasses (or hold camera toward the material if fit is off).
2. **The moment (10s):** Have 3 laminated props ready — a worksheet with a paragraph, a menu, a handwritten note. Visitor presses the button. Glasses read it aloud. *Let the audio finish — silence sells it.*
3. **Twist (10s):** "Now double-press." Scene description of the booth/visitor. Always gets a reaction.
4. **Story (20s):** "This started as a science fair project for visually impaired students who can't read handouts in class. Commercial devices like this cost $2,000–3,000. This is under $60 in parts. Every classroom could afford a set."
5. **Close (15s):** "I'm taking preorders today — scan this. First 30 kits ship next month." Point at QR.

Tips: laminate the props (sweaty hands), keep readings SHORT (a 3-sentence prop = 15s demo, not 90s), let *them* press the button — ownership sells. If a line forms, run the scene-describe demo on the crowd itself.

## Pitch answers you'll need

- "How does it work?" → Camera + Raspberry Pi; a deliberate button press sends the image or speech to OpenAI, and the answer comes back in an AI-generated voice. AI features need internet, so keep a phone hotspot ready at the booth.
- "Battery life?" → "Runs all day off a pocket power bank — a 5,000mAh bank is ~6+ hours. The production kit integrates the battery into the frame."
- "Is it private?" → Nothing is stored; press-to-capture only, no continuous recording. (Say this proactively — a camera on glasses at a con WILL get this question.)
- "Latency?" → Usually 4–6 seconds on a solid Wi-Fi or hotspot connection; AI actions do not run offline.
- "Why not a phone app?" → Hands-free, head-aimed, no aiming a phone you can't see; built for a classroom workflow.

## Preorders — realistic framing

You have parts ×30 but zero units built. **Sell "founder kits", not finished medical devices:**

- Offer: **$99 Founder Kit preorder** — all components + printed frame + preloaded SD card + assembly guide, "ships within 6 weeks." Parts cost ~$55–60, so margin covers your time, failed prints, shipping.
- Or two tiers: $79 kit (you solder) / $149 assembled.
- Collect **intent, not money**, unless you have a real checkout: a Google Form / Tally form via QR (name, email, tier, quantity) is zero-risk. If you want payment, Stripe Payment Link with "reserve for $20 deposit, refundable."
- **Do not market it as a medical/assistive device with claims** ("restores reading ability") — sell it as an open-source DIY AI wearable kit *inspired by* an accessibility project. Keeps you clear of device-regulation and liability issues while telling the same story.
- Mention each kit needs the buyer's own API key (or include "3 months of cloud reads included" and eat ~$2/user).

## Booth checklist

- [ ] QR sign (form link) — print 2, tape one flat to the table
- [ ] 3 laminated demo props (worksheet, menu, handwritten note)
- [ ] One-pager handout ×50: photo, how it works diagram, price, QR
- [ ] Demo video on phone/tablet on loop (backup + crowd magnet)
- [ ] Display unit with labeled guts ("this is everything — $60")
- [ ] Two charged power banks + spare cable + spare cloned SD card + toolkit
- [ ] Phone hotspot ON, glasses pre-joined
- [ ] Business cards or at least the QR on your phone lock screen

## Sunday follow-up (don't skip)

Email every form signup within 24h while they remember you: thank you, one great photo, firm price, payment link, ship date. Conference leads decay fast.
