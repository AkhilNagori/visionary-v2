# Visionary — Maximum Feature Universe

Premise: camera + mic + speaker + WiFi + frontier AI, worn on your face. That's not a gadget, that's an ambient computer. Everything below is buildable on the current hardware (exceptions marked). ★ = demoable Saturday with a prompt change.

## Communication (the phone-replacement core)
1. Dictate & send texts/emails by voice (via paired phone or WiFi APIs)
2. Hear incoming messages read aloud, reply hands-free
3. VoIP calls — mic + speaker + WiFi is literally a phone (echo cancellation is the work)
4. Voice memos to self / to others
5. Notification triage — "anything important?" → AI summarizes your inbox, skips the noise
6. "Read me my feeds" — news/RSS/newsletters as a spoken briefing

## Can it replace a phone? The honest pitch
What a phone does: communicate, navigate, capture, consume, pay, doomscroll.
- Communicate: yes — voice-first (1–6).
- Capture: yes — better; it's your POV.
- Navigate: partly (needs GPS via paired phone or a $10 GPS module in v3).
- Consume: audio yes (podcasts, briefings); video no — and that's the point.
- Pay: no (and shouldn't).
- Doomscroll: **impossible. No screen. That's the feature.**

Booth line: *"It won't replace your phone. It replaces the 200 times a day you pull it out for ten seconds — and gives your eyes and hands back. A phone shows you the world through a screen; this tells you about the world you're already looking at."*

Hardware honesty: no cellular (WiFi/hotspot), no GPS, no IMU (no fall detection/step counting) — all are cheap v3 modules. Everything else above runs on what's on the bench right now.
